
Problem 

The Analog Legacy: 90% of global health history (especially in the Global South) is handwritten, multi-lingual, and physically archived on paper.

 Current "off-the-shelf" AI pipelines (Standard OCR + RAG) are failing at the institutional level because they are "Spatial-Blind.” And  OCR inaccuracy.  When a doctor writes a diagnosis across two columns, or a pharmacy stamp overlaps a policy number, standard systems commit two fatal errors:

Serialization Gore: The OCR reads horizontally across the page, mixing two different patients' data or merging a price with a date, creating a "meaningless soup" of text.

Contextual Hallucination: Because the RAG system doesn't "see" the paper, it retrieves the most semantically similar policy (e.g., a 2025 standard plan) even if the handwritten claim explicitly refers to an Obsolete 2018 Rider. The AI "hallucinates" a denial because it’s looking at the wrong map.

 

The context

We have a dataset of 100k complex insurance claims. These aren't clean PDFs; they are multilingual (Arabic/English) scans of handwritten/illegible doctor notes, receipts with overlapping stamps, and inconsistent policy references.

 Our current pipeline fails because the OCR extracts text in the wrong order and not the best accuracy  (reading across columns instead of down), and the RAG system retrieves "Standard data " chunks when the claim actually refers to an "Obsolete 2018 Rider."
